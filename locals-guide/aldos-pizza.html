Aldo's Pizza

If you're searching for a charming spot in Green Bay that's been delighting locals for over 50 years, look no further than Aldo's Pizza. Established in 1969, this cozy eatery has earned a loyal following with its unique offerings. Let's take a closer look at my recent visit to Aldo's Pizza.

As we pulled up to the location, it resembled an old-fashioned house-turned-restaurant. The exterior didn't immediately give away its culinary treasures, and if I hadn't been determined to try it, I might have overlooked it. But we ventured inside and found a cozy dining area that efficiently used its space to accommodate guests. Seating ourselves, we noticed the quirky decor adorned with memorabilia of The Three Stooges â€“ an interesting and unexpected touch.

We ordered a classic Wisconsin treat, a plate of cheese curds, and Aldo's famed 14x17 pizza, well-known among locals. The cheese curds were delicious, as expected, but the pizza was a unique surprise. Unlike the usual circular pizzas, Aldo's serves their scrumptious creation in a rectangular shape. The crust was thin and flaky, and they generously loaded it with pepperoni and cheese, creating a savory delight that left us wanting more.

However, one peculiarity stood out during our visit. Even though we dined in, the restaurant provided us with just one small styrofoam plate each, only big enough to hold a single slice of their square pizza. While not a deal-breaker, it was an odd aspect of our experience.

All in all, Aldo's Pizza offers great food, but I'm not sure it's a place I'd frequent regularly. When seeking an extraordinary pizza experience, I'd personally lean towards Rustique Pizzeria. Nevertheless, I wouldn't hesitate to enjoy Aldo's Pizza again if the opportunity presented itself.

Next time you're in Green Bay and on the hunt for a taste of local history, head over to Aldo's Pizza.
