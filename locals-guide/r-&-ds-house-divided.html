R & D's House Divided

Discover R & D House Divided, a unique Packers Bears bar situated on University Ave in Green Bay. With one owner being a Packers fan and the other a Bears fan, the concept intrigued me, prompting a visit to this intriguing spot.

Upon entering R & D House Divided, you'll find a warm and welcoming atmosphere with various tables and a bar at the front. Unlike traditional restaurants, here, you can choose your own table without waiting to be seated. The walls are adorned with a delightful mix of Packers and Bears memorabilia, creating the perfect ambiance for sports enthusiasts. While there seems to be a slight preference for Packers items, it's only fitting given the bar's location in Green Bay.

During our visit, we were greeted by an incredibly friendly bartender who exuded enthusiasm, making us feel right at home. Our waitress promptly took our drink orders, and to our surprise, we were served R & D House Divided bottles of water. This unique touch of branding was interesting, although it's worth noting that the water is only available in $2 bottles rather than traditional glasses.

Moving on to the food, I opted for the Italian beef sandwich, expecting a taste reminiscent of the ones served at renowned Chicago spots like Portillo's or Taste of the Windy City (formerly in Green Bay). However, the sandwich's beef was cut a bit thicker and piled on rather than thinly sliced, deviating from the classic style. Despite this, the sandwich was still enjoyable. In general, the food received a decent rating from our group of four, but nothing particularly stood out.

R & D House Divided unquestionably presents a fascinating concept, but in terms of the culinary experience, it might not have left a lasting impression on us. Personally, I might prefer heading further down the road to Legend Larry's for a meal. However, one aspect that did shine during our visit was the wonderful waitress who made the experience delightful with her cheerful demeanor and quick service. If you're looking to enjoy a drink with your Bears fan friend, this bar might be worth considering, but as far as the food goes, there might be other options worth exploring.
