1919 Kitchen and Tap

Step into 1919 Kitchen & Tap, a delightful restaurant nestled within the iconic Lambeau Field, offering a refreshing change from Curly's with its updated ambiance. This family-friendly spot boasts a spacious bar with TV screens in every direction, making it the perfect spot to unwind, catch the game, and savor some of Wisconsin's finest craft beer. However, when it comes to the culinary experience, there are some aspects to consider.

The restaurant's atmosphere is undeniably captivating. Unlike Curly's Pub, 1919 Kitchen & Tap seamlessly blends in with Lambeau Field's aesthetics, complementing its surroundings beautifully. As you take in the sights, you'll notice TV screens and even get a glimpse of the impressive 50-foot Lombardi trophy, adding to the excitement.

For beer enthusiasts, 1919 Kitchen & Tap boasts a diverse drink menu, with a strong emphasis on craft beers. With an extensive selection available, you can explore various flavors while enjoying sports on one of their numerous TVs. However, the dining experience leaves room for improvement.

While the cheese curds deserve praise for their delectable taste, the rest of the menu fell short of expectations. The food quality has been inconsistent, with items often arriving cold or prepared incorrectly. Furthermore, the service might be slow during busy times, adding to the frustration. Considering the prices, which tend to be on the higher side due to its tourist attraction status, the overall value for money might not be satisfactory.

In conclusion, 1919 Kitchen & Tap impresses with its attractive design and inviting ambiance. It's an excellent spot to gather for drinks and enjoy the company. However, if you're seeking a satisfying dining experience, you might want to explore other options in the Green Bay area, such as Rustique Pizzeria or Copper State Brewing, which offer better quality at more reasonable prices. Additionally, keep in mind that during game day weekends, the restaurant can get overwhelmingly busy.
