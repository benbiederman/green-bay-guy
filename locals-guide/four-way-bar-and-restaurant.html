Four-way Bar and Restaurant

Four-Way Bar & Restaurant, situated in Suamico, has gained popularity for its renowned Friday fish fry. This establishment, operating since 1978 and proudly family-owned, is a delightful combination of a bar and restaurant (as suggested by the name). With a diverse array of dishes on their menu, Four-Way Bar & Restaurant caters to a wide range of tastes, ensuring there's something appealing for every diner.

When it comes to describing my experience at the restaurant, "cluster" is the word that immediately comes to mind. Upon arriving at the bustling location, I approached the hostess desk but ended up waiting for around five minutes before someone noticed me and inquired about my needs. I expressed my interest in getting a table and was informed that I needed to speak with the bartenders to obtain a number, which would determine the order of service.

Navigating through the crowded bar, I finally managed to get my number after a few more minutes of waiting. I settled for a small table in the bar area and eagerly awaited my turn. The service proceeded based on the assigned numbers, so when my turn came up, a waitress came over to take my order. The food was promptly brought to the table, but to my surprise, there were no drinks accompanying the meal. It was then that I discovered I should have placed the drink order at the bar instead of through the waitress.

After finishing my meal, a while had passed, and I was still waiting for my bill. At this point, I approached the person who had taken my food order to inquire about it. To my chagrin, she directed me to settle the bill at the bar. I had to make my way back to the already crowded bar area to have the bartenders handle the payment.

Reflecting on the entire process, I can't help but feel exhausted just recounting it all over again.

Regarding the food, it was decent overall. The wings, in particular, stood out as they had a unique twist – being breaded like fried chicken – which turned out to be better than I had initially anticipated.

However, one aspect that left me a bit surprised was the price. I ordered 12 wings at 50 cents each according to the menu, along with fries and a coke. Logically, the bill should have come to a total of $6 for the wings plus the cost of fries and the drink. But to my astonishment, my bill ended up being $16, which didn't align with my calculations.

Despite feeling unsure about the total, I hesitated to question it at the time. The bartenders were swamped with their tasks, trying to handle multiple customers' orders and payments simultaneously. As a result, I felt a bit awkward about raising the issue and opted to pay the amount indicated on the bill without further inquiry.
