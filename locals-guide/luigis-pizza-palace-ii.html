Luigi's Pizza Palace II

Welcome to Luigi's Pizza Palace II, conveniently situated just outside Green Bay in the charming town of Little Suamico. Don't let the short drive discourage you from experiencing the culinary delights of this exceptional restaurant. As a family-owned establishment, Luigi's Pizza Palace II has been serving up mouthwatering pizzas since 1986.

When visiting Luigi's Pizza Palace II, be prepared for a brief wait to be seated. Arriving early on a Friday evening, my party and I patiently waited for about 10 minutes. The wait, though not excessive, grew longer as the night progressed. Interestingly, Luigi's Pizza Palace II has gained a reputation for its delectable fish fry, an unexpected specialty for a pizza joint. Glancing around the restaurant, it was evident that a majority of diners were savoring the fish. However, on my inaugural visit to Luigi's Pizza Palace II, I had come solely for their pizza. And oh, what a treat it was!

In addition to ordering pizza, we decided to sample their beer nugget appetizers. Beer nuggets are savory dough chunks, deep-fried and sprinkled with a delightful seasoning. Served with a tangy tomato dipping sauce, these beer nuggets were priced at an affordable $4.99. I mention the price because the portion size was astonishingly generous. The beer nuggets arrived in a bucket, leaving me astounded. In fact, my wife and I could have easily foregone the pizza and filled up solely on these delicious nuggets.

Once we had indulged in an abundance of beer nuggets, our eagerly awaited pizzas were served. To my pleasant surprise, the pizza surpassed my expectations. While one can never be certain about the quality of pizza at a new establishment, Luigi's Pizza Palace II knows how to craft a truly remarkable pie. The pizza was generously topped with an abundance of cheese and made with premium ingredients. What truly impressed me, though, was the flavor-packed pepperoni. Whether it was the pizza's cooking method or the quality of the pepperoni itself, the taste was exceptionally vibrant. The pepperoni had been expertly cooked to a delightful crispness.

Frankly, having experienced Luigi's Pizza Palace II firsthand, it comes as no surprise that they have flourished since 1986. The portion sizes offered at such reasonable prices are truly remarkable. I will undoubtedly be returning to Luigi's Pizza Palace II in the future.

One final note worth mentioning is the possibility that the same family operates Luigi's Italian Bistro in Green Bay. The menus of both establishments are strikingly similar, and the food is nearly identical. Rest assured, whether you choose one or the other, you will be pleased with your decision.
